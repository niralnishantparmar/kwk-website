Our Mission

Why Women in STEM?

STEM (science, technology, engineering, and math) fields are shaping the future. While there are barriers, there are ways to remedy them. With a focus on inclusivity, providing spaces for everyone’s voices to be heard, and amplifying diversity, women can find their place in STEM. Everyone deserves a seat at the table—especially women.

What’s the Problem?

There is a significant lack of access to education for women around the globe, which has led to the gender gap in STEM fields. The conversation surrounding technology is constantly changing, and it is seen as the thing that will change our future as we know it. With something that holds increasing importance in our society, why shouldn’t women have a say in it?

By the Numbers

  • Women made up only 28.2% of the global STEM workforce in 2024. (SWE)
  • Only 35% of STEM graduates over the past 10 years were women. (UNESCO)

Other barriers include stereotypes, unconscious bias, lack of role models, and work-life balance struggles. (MIT)

Why Do We Care?

When tasked with creating data visualizations on a topic we are passionate about, we all knew we wanted to focus on women. As Kode with Klossy scholars, an organization dedicated to introducing young women and gender non conforming teenagers to the world of code, this topic was very close to us.

Why Should You Care?

Not only is this something that affects us, it’s something that affects you too! With an increase in diversity, there’s also an increase in different perspectives which can provide important tools, new ways of understanding problems, and the skills to solve particular challenges. Ensuring women’s voices are heard in STEM helps us all move forward.
